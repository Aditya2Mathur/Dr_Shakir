Back Pain

Expert care for
back pain relief

Back pain is one of the most common health problems seen in people of all age groups. It may occur due to injuries, poor posture, heavy lifting, or underlying medical conditions. In younger people, it can often be linked to muscle strain or sudden movement, while in older people it may be related to conditions like disc problems, arthritis, or degeneration of the spine.

Lower back pain is usually connected to the bones of the lumbar spine, spinal cord, or supporting ligaments. Upper back pain, on the other hand, may be caused by issues such as inflammation, abnormal growths, or other medical concerns in the chest and spine.

Neck Pain

Advanced treatment for
neck pain problems

Neck pain is a very common problem that affects people of all ages. It may happen due to poor posture, long hours of sitting, sudden jerks, muscle strain, or injuries. In some cases, neck pain can also be linked to problems in the bones, nerves, or discs of the cervical spine. For many patients, the pain can cause stiffness, reduced movement, and discomfort in daily activities.

To identify the exact cause of neck pain, doctors may suggest tests such as X-rays, MRI scans, or other imaging methods. With the right diagnosis and treatment, most cases of neck pain can be managed effectively through medicines.

Sciatica

Sciatica pain relief
with expert care

Sciatica is a condition where the sciatic nerve, which runs from the lower back down to the legs, becomes pressed or irritated. This nerve is the largest in the body, and when it gets affected, it causes pain that usually starts in the lower back and travels through the hips, thighs, and sometimes even below the knee.

Apart from pain, patients with sciatica may also experience numbness, tingling, or weakness in the leg. The condition is commonly caused by problems like a slipped disc, bone spurs, or narrowing of the spinal canal.

Tumor of Bone and Nerves

Tumor of bones and nerves
treated with precision

Tumors of the bones and nerves occur when abnormal cells start growing in an uncontrolled way. These tumors can replace healthy tissues, making the bone weaker and more likely to fracture. In the early stages, many bone and nerve tumors are non-cancerous, but if left untreated, they may grow further and cause serious problems.

Treatment often involves tumor excision surgery, where the doctor carefully removes the affected part. In some cases, medicines or additional therapies may also be advised depending on the type and stage of the tumor.
